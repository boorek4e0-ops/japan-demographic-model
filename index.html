Demographic transition model

Left Y-axis: crude birth (blue) & death (red) per 1,000. Right Y-axis: population (green, millions). Amber line: 2026 (~now). Purple: 2050. White dashed: your selected year, with horizontal guides to all three series.

Stage 2 → 3 (1950s–1970s) Death rates fall from moderate to low; birth rates still relatively high → strong population growth and “demographic dividend” potential.
Stage 3 (≈1970–1990s) Birth rates fall toward death rates; growth slows as fertility declines (second demographic transition).
Stage 4 → 5 (2000s onward) Low birth, rising death (aging); natural decrease; population peaks then declines—classic late transition / post-transition pattern in projections.
